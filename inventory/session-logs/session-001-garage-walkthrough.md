## DECLUTTER FLAGS

### Items to Challenge:

1. **Detergent jugs (5+)** — Do you really need 5+ jugs of detergent? That's easily a year's supply. Keep 2, use the rest first, stop buying more until these are gone.

2. **Multiple WD-40 variants (3+)** — You have regular WD-40, silicone spray, AND pump-style. Do you need all three formulations? The standard WD-40 handles most tasks. Keep 1-2, ditch the duplicates.

3. **3 spray paint cans (white, green, purple)** — Are these for a specific project? If they've been in storage for a year+ with no plan, they may have lost pressure. Test them — if they don't spray well, trash them.

4. **Instruction manuals/documentation** — Most of these are available online as PDFs. Unless it's for a very old/obscure item, recycle the paper.

5. **3 sleeping bags** — Family of 3 (with a 1.5-year-old). Do you need 3 adult-sized sleeping bags? Unless you regularly host camping guests, 2 should cover you. The kid has his own setup eventually.

6. **Kayak paddles (3)** — Same question. 3 paddles for 2 adults? Unless they're different types (kayak vs canoe), consider whether you need all 3.

7. **Snowboard + parts box** — When's the last time you actually went snowboarding? Colorado Springs has access, sure, but if this has been sitting in storage for 2+ years untouched, be honest with yourself. Rentals are easy.

8. **Framed prints (multiple)** — Are these going on walls, or are they going to sit in a box in the garage for another 2 years? If you don't have wall space planned for them, they're just taking up storage.

9. **Pink decorative item with pom-poms** — What even is this? If you can't immediately explain it, it goes.

10. **CVS pharmacy bag** — This is literally a shopping bag in your storage. Trash it.
